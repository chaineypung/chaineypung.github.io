Weaving Light and Time: Unified Harmonic-Geometric Representation Learning for Dense RGB-Event Parsing

Evita is a unified RGB-Event backbone for robust dense perception in motion blur, low light, adverse weather, and other challenging real-world scenes.

Chenxu Peng Chongtian Zhou Dicheng Liu Bowen Yin Yimian Dai Xialei Liu Ming-Ming Cheng Xiang Li
NKIARI, Shenzhen Futian · VCIP, CS, Nankai University · AAIS, Nankai University

Concept

Evita starts from a simple observation: RGB frames and event streams are complementary, but they are not naturally aligned in space, time, or representation. The framework therefore addresses dataset alignment, event-format pretraining, and unified RGB-Event feature learning together.

Architecture

The Evita block embeds geometric, spectral, and routing operations inside the encoder itself. This makes RGB-Event interaction an intrinsic part of representation learning rather than a late-stage decoder operation.

Geometric Alignment

Geometric Parallax Rectification predicts bounded offsets and masks to align event features with RGB structure.

Spectral Transfer

Harmonic Spectral Resonance moves event-driven high-frequency cues through the frequency domain while preserving RGB phase information.

Event-Guided Routing

Transient Global Routing uses event features as dynamic queries to gather global photometric context from the RGB stream.

Qualitative and Efficiency Results

Across night, fog, cloudy, rainy, and sunny scenes, Evita produces cleaner dense parsing around thin structures, road boundaries, vehicles, and low-visibility regions. The qualitative comparison highlights that event cues are most useful when RGB alone becomes unreliable.

The accuracy-FLOPs curve further shows why a unified backbone matters: Evita reaches strong mIoU while avoiding the redundant computation of large dual-stream pipelines.
